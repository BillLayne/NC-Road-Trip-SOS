The Five Costliest Labor Day Insurance Mistakes

The biggest threat on the road is another driver with little or no insurance. Your minimum Uninsured Motorist (UM) coverage is a fraction of what a serious accident costs, leaving your family to cover a massive financial gap.

Financial Impact of a Disabling Injury

Total accident cost: $155,000. Your minimum coverage pays only $50,000.

Your Out-of-Pocket Gap: $105,000

Immediate Actions:

  • Contact your agent and get a quote to increase your UM/UIM limits to at least $100k/$300k, or to match your liability coverage.
  • Ask about increasing Uninsured Motorist Property Damage (UMPD) to cover hit-and-run vehicle repairs without a deductible.

Relying on your personal auto policy or credit card for a rental car is risky. They often don't cover common fees like "Loss of Use" or "Diminution of Value," leaving you with a surprise bill after a minor accident.

After a minor parking lot accident in a rental:

Immediate Actions:

  • Call your agent and ask if your policy covers "Loss of Use" and "Diminution of Value" for rentals.
  • Review your credit card's benefits guide to understand its limitations.
  • Budget for and consider buying the rental company's Collision Damage Waiver (CDW/LDW) for peace of mind.

Your homeowners policy offers limited coverage for belongings away from home, and it likely excludes liability for incidents at a vacation rental you host (e.g., an Airbnb).

Theft from Your Car

Your homeowners coverage for "off-premises" property is often just 10% of your total limit, with even lower sub-limits for electronics. A $7,000 theft could result in a payout of only $1,500 after deductibles and limits.

The Airbnb Lawsuit

If a guest is injured at your rental property, your standard homeowners policy will likely deny the claim due to a "business activity" exclusion, leaving you personally liable for hundreds of thousands of dollars.

Immediate Actions:

  • Check your homeowners policy for "off-premises" coverage limits and sub-limits for valuables.
  • If you host a rental, get a specific Short-Term Rental (STR) insurance policy immediately.
  • Request a quote for a $1 million personal umbrella policy to protect your assets from major lawsuits.

Basic roadside assistance plans have towing limits (often just 15 miles) that are inadequate for rural or mountain travel, leaving you stranded with a hefty bill.
